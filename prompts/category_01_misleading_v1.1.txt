CASCADING CLASSIFICATION (5 Levels — evaluate top-down, exit as soon as a level resolves)

2. Level 0 + 1: Content Gate & Claim Detection

Level 0 — CONTENT GATE: Is this reviewable marketing content?
- If the content is not an "advertisement" per § 275.206(4)-1(e)(1) → return []
- Excluded: statutory/regulatory filings, extemporaneous live oral communications,
  unsolicited one-on-one hypothetical performance responses
- If excluded → return []

Level 1 — CLAIM DETECTION: Does the content contain flaggable claims?
- Scan for statements about performance, benefits, superiority, outcomes,
  recommendations, risk, fees, or investment process
- If NO such claims exist → return []
- If claims found → proceed to Level 2 (Step 3)

3. Level 2: Pre-Filter — Overrides, Safe Harbors & Materiality Gate
Before classifying any candidate issue, run it through this three-stage filter IN ORDER.

3a. OVERRIDE CHECK (runs FIRST — these always flag)
If the candidate matches ANY override pattern below, SKIP safe harbors and materiality — proceed directly to Issue Detection (Step 4).

OVR-1: Performance Superlatives
  Keywords: "superior performance", "outperformed", "exceptional results",
            "beat the market", "exceeded expectations", "strong returns",
            "top-tier performance"
  Rule: Performance superlatives require substantiation regardless of nearby hedging.
  Example: "We seek to deliver superior performance." → FLAG (hedge does not protect performance superlatives)

OVR-2: Guarantee Language
  Keywords: "guaranteed", "guarantee", "assured", "certain", "definitely",
            "cannot lose", "risk-free"
  Rule: Guarantee language is prohibited in investment context. Always flag.
  Example: "Returns are virtually guaranteed." → FLAG

OVR-3: Unqualified Return / Alpha Claims
  Keywords: "generated alpha", "delivered outperformance", "produced returns",
            "contributed to superior performance"
  Rule: Past performance claims without substantiation (benchmark, period, source) always flag.
  Example: "Our strategy generated alpha consistently." → FLAG

→ If ANY OVR pattern matches → go to Step 4. Do NOT apply safe harbors.

3b. SAFE HARBOR PATTERNS (suppress if matched, no OVR triggered)
If the candidate does NOT match any OVR pattern, check these safe harbors. If matched → do not flag.

SH-1: Hedged / Aspirational Language
  Signals: "seek to", "aims to", "may", "might", "could", "potentially",
           "designed to", "intended to", "expected to", "believes",
           "anticipates", "strive to"
  Rationale: Hedge words reduce certainty — describes intent, not promised outcome.
  Example: "We seek to capitalize on opportunities." → NO FLAG

SH-2: Capability / Resource Descriptions
  Signals: "team of X", "offices in", "platform", "infrastructure",
           "resources", "large research teams", "deep expertise"
  Rationale: Factual statements about firm resources ≠ performance promises.
  Example: "Our team of 50 analysts covers global markets." → NO FLAG

SH-3: Operational Superlatives
  Signals: superlative + "systems" / "infrastructure" / "service" /
           "technology" / "process" / "risk management"
  Rationale: Superlatives about operations ≠ performance claims.
  Example: "World-class risk management systems." → NO FLAG

SH-4: Quoted / Branded Terms
  Signals: text in quotation marks that appears to be a product name,
           strategy name, or internal terminology
  Rationale: Branded names are identifiers, not substantive claims.
  Example: "'Best ideas in global credit'" as a strategy name → NO FLAG

SH-5: Substantiated Claims
  Signals: source citation, time period, methodology, gross/net specification
           all present alongside the claim
  Rationale: Properly substantiated claims are compliant.
  Example: "Ranked #1 by Barron's for 2024 based on risk-adjusted returns." → NO FLAG

CRITICAL CHECK before suppressing:
  "Is this claim about INVESTMENT PERFORMANCE/OUTCOMES, or about BUSINESS OPERATIONS/PROCESS?"
  → If OPERATIONS/PROCESS with hedging → suppress (no flag)
  → If PERFORMANCE/OUTCOMES → safe harbor does NOT apply; proceed to 3c

→ If safe harbor matched AND no OVR triggered → do not flag. Move to next candidate.

3c. MATERIALITY GATE (reasonable investor test)
For claims that survived overrides (no OVR match) and safe harbors (no SH match):

Ask: "Would a reasonable investor consider this statement material to their investment decision?"

IMMATERIAL — do not flag:
  • Generic marketing puffery about non-investment topics
  • Administrative or operational boilerplate
  • Statements with no bearing on performance, risk, returns, fees, or outcomes
  Example: "We are committed to excellence in client service." → NO FLAG (puffery)

MATERIAL — proceed to Issue Detection (Step 4):
  • Relates to performance, risk, returns, fees, investment process, or outcomes
  • Could influence a reasonable investor's decision
  Example: "Our proprietary process consistently identifies undervalued securities." → MATERIAL (proceed)

PROCESSING ORDER SUMMARY:
  Candidate → OVR check → (if OVR match: go to Step 4)
                        → (if no OVR: SH check)
                            → (if SH match: suppress, no flag)
                            → (if no SH: Materiality gate)
                                → (if immaterial: suppress, no flag)
                                → (if material: go to Step 4)

WHY THIS ORDER:
- Promissory FIRST: explicit promises are the clearest, highest-severity violation.
- Implied Guarantees SECOND: softer version of promissory — indirect certainty.
- Overstated THIRD: specific language triggers (superlatives) are easy to identify.
- Unsubstantiated FOURTH: requires checking whether evidence exists in context.
- Unbalanced FIFTH: presentation problem, not about the claim's truth.
- Ambiguous LAST: catch-all — should only trigger when nothing more specific fits.

5. Category Scope (Gate 6)
Flag here issues about a claim’s truthfulness, substantiation, balance, or misleading impression, including:
Superlatives without substantiation
Promises of outcomes
Absolute claims
Benefits without balanced risk disclosure
Vague claims missing essential context
Qualifier removal implying certainty

Key rule: If the issue is about a claim’s truthfulness, substantiation, or balance—flag in this category.

6. Recommendation phrasing
Use these templates based on the matched theme.sub_buckets[*].name:
* Unsubstantiated Statements of Fact: "Superlative/superiority claim is unsubstantiated and may create a misleading impression. Please remove or provide supporting detail (ranking source, criteria, applicable years)."
* Promissory or Certain Outcome Language: "Use of 'guarantee/guaranteed' implies certainty of outcome and is misleading in an investment context. Please remove and revise to non-promissory language."
* Overstated or Absolute Claims: "Absolute ranking/award claim lacks a disclosed measurement period and may mislead readers regarding current status. Please specify applicable years or remove."
* Unbalanced Benefit Statements: "Language implies guaranteed or effortless investment outcomes and is misleading. Please remove or revise to eliminate any implication of guaranteed results and add proportional risk disclosure."
* Ambiguous or Vague Claims: "Third-party award/ranking content is presented without clear source attribution, which may mislead readers regarding the origin and nature of the recognition. Please add explicit source citation."
* Implied Guarantees or Certainty: "Qualifying language was removed, increasing certainty and creating potentially misleading claims. Please restore hedging language ('may', 'expect', 'could') to reflect uncertainty."
